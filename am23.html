What is it?

A solar-powered chemical reactor that:

1. Uses sunlight and a small voltage to break down PET plastic waste into valuable chemicals (mainly glycolic acid).

2. Simultaneously produces hydrogen gas (H‚ÇÇ), a clean fuel, from water.

Core idea

1. Instead of burning or landfilling plastic (which causes pollution), this system upcycles plastic waste into useful products.

2. It combines photocatalysis (light-driven reactions) and electrochemistry in one device.

Why it's important or needed?

1. It solves two problems at once, i.e., plastic pollution and clean energy demand.

2. It is a green process as it is powered by sunlight and doesn't emit anything toxic.
